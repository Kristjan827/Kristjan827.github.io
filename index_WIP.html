01 — Work

Problems solved in the field

Problem: alignment without reference
Steel Column Layout — AnyDesk Screen Mirror

40 steel columns needed placing with ironworkers needing live position reference but no way to read survey instruments. Mirrored the total station screen via AnyDesk — ironworkers could see their offset in real time without a second person on the instrument.

40 columns, 4 hours, zeros on as-built
Problem: IFC model producing blade instability
LandXML Surface QC Tool

Near-vertical triangles in IFC-converted surfaces caused 3D machine control blade shake on a Leica MCP80 setup. Built an interactive HTML QC tool and a Civil 3D LISP routine (BADTRI) to flag and remove degenerate faces before export.

Blade instability resolved, clean surface exported
Problem: volume reporting on active excavation
Dual-Altitude UAV Survey — Equinox Processing

816-image dual-altitude photogrammetry mission over active earthworks. Processed in Metashape on Equinox workstation — produced ortho, point cloud, and volume comparison against design surface. Turnaround under one working day.

Client volume report delivered same day
Problem: five bridge layouts, tight tolerances
Rail Baltica Bridge Control

Layout and as-built measurement for five bridge structures on the Rail Baltica main line. Geodetic control established from national network, deformation monitoring, and as-built documentation against design IFC packages.

All structures within design tolerance
Problem: subgrade QC on remote machine control site
IFC-to-TIN LISP — BricsCAD BatchFacesToTIN

Existing workflow required manual extraction of 3D solids from IFC models for machine control surface preparation. Built a BricsCAD LISP routine (BatchFacesToTIN) to batch-convert faces to TIN breaklines and boundary polylines automatically.

Hours of manual work reduced to minutes
Problem: site data scattered across platforms
NanoClaw — Site Management System

Built a full-stack internal site reporting system (FastAPI + PostgreSQL + React) running on a Raspberry Pi 5 via Cloudflare Tunnel. WhatsApp Business API integration for field reporting, Remato import, supplier delivery tracking, and PM dashboard.

In active production use
02 — Fieldbook

A guide to field judgment

Five chapters on the decisions that don't appear in instrument manuals — the judgment calls, workarounds, and communication patterns that close the gap between what's designed and what gets built.

Chapter 01
The Information Delivery Problem

On giving people data in the format they can actually act on — not just accurate, but actionable. The AnyDesk story.

Chapter 02
Reading the Machine

What blade behavior tells you about the surface model before you check the file. Diagnosing 3D machine control issues from the cab signal alone.

Chapter 03
Cross-Vendor Geometry

Leica, Topcon, and Trimble speak different dialects. Coordinate system traps, file format translation, and the checks that catch silent errors.

Chapter 04
Stockpile and Volume Discipline

Why volume numbers lie and how to make them honest. Survey timing, surface selection, and the conversation to have before the excavator starts.

Chapter 05
The Setup That Saves the Day

Field ergonomics and instrument setup decisions that seem minor until something goes wrong. The habits that compress recovery time.

Thesis
Measurement is a means, not an end

The surveyor's job isn't to collect data. It's to deliver information in a form the recipient can act on — and to know the difference.
